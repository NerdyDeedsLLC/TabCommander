"What is TabCommander?"
Got WAY too many tabs open? How about browser windows? Can't find the tab you were JUST staring at? Maybe you're done shopping and you want ALL of your Amazon and eBay tabs closed? No problem.

TabCommander is a powerful tab management extension for Chrome. But don't take my word for it:


Using nothing but a few keystrokes in the OmniBox, users gain godlike command-line abilities to search, sort, group, consolidate, extract, and discard any number of open tabs (from all open windows, or just the active one)!

Tab Commander leverages the search capabilities and power of Regular Expressions, so you can instantly filter their open tabs (scanning both page titles and domains) and effect actions on them en masse, all without leaving the current page!

  2. ‚ùóÔ∏è Logical 'NOT': Prefacing a search term with a bang (!) will EXCLUDE any results containing it instead of including them.

    Example I:
    !youtube will gather all tabs EXCEPT those containing youtube in their title or URL.

    Even cooler, though, is you can mix and match includes and excludes! Each Logical OR (|) is considered a separate search term, so you can negate one or many while requiring one or many, too!

    Example II:
    google|yahoo|!bing is the equivalent of saying "find everything containing "google" or "yahoo" (so long as it DOESN'T contain "bing").

    Not exclusions take precidence over inclusions, so you can use them to further refine your results if you're still getting too many, too!

    Example III:
    hammer|saw|wrench|!mc will return the tools in the garage, but exclude the "too legit" tool from the 90's, even though mc appears after hammer.

    Only want words that match your term entirely instead of including substrings (e.g. when searching for "laughter", don't show "slaughter" as well)? If you wrap a query or term in double-quotes (") then SimplEx treats it as you searching for the word so wrapped in its entirety

FILTERING WITH REGEX

"Screw 'SimplEx'; How do I use REGEX?"
True RegEx Mode was designed to be as comfortable and intuitive to access for developers as possible:

Simply type tab, to initialize TabCommander, like you would for any other query, but this time, after you hit the spacebar (but before entering a query) type a forward slash (/). That's it. You're searching with RegEx!

True RegEx Mode runs your query precisely as you enter it, using JavaScript/ECMA syntax:
/^http:\/\//, for example, would return insecure (those not running in https) tabs only.
/(?!.*tube)(?<=.*google.*)(you\b)/i will return https://LetMeGoogleThatForYou.com (as well as https://lmgtfy.com, since they spell out their whole name in the page title), but will omit your youtube.com tabs and ignore the socks you're looking at on Amazon (since both fail the positive lookbehind for 'google').

And yes: the /i and /g flags are supported, as are (pos and neg) lookaheads, lookbehinds, capture groups (dunno why you'd care in this case) and all the other ECMARE goodness you've come to expect.
